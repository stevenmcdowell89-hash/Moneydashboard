# Money Dashboard — Guide

One screen. Your money at a glance, editable in place, with a live forecast and
savings targets that react the instant you change a number.

## The home screen is everything

There's no separate "plan editor" or "dashboard" — the landing screen **is** both.
It auto-saves continuously (the engine runs in your browser, so every change
recomputes instantly; the small "Saved" pill confirms it persisted).

Top to bottom:

- **Hero** — the big number is **Left to spend this month** (income − bills −
  savings − one-offs). Below it: money **In**, **Bills**, **Saved**. Tap
  **Cash now** to set your current balance. The ⚙️ opens Settings.
- **Forecast** — cash + savings projected forward (1y / 2y / 3y). Target hit-months
  are marked; the red dashed line is zero (going below = trouble). Months are real
  ("Mar 2027"), based on today.
- **Try a what-if** — a toggle that turns every income/outgoing into a slider.
  Drag them and the forecast, target dates and end-cash move live, showing the
  delta ("↓ 2 mo sooner", "+£140 end cash", "back in the black"). Save it as a
  named scenario, or reset. Saved what-ifs appear as chips to reload.
- **Targets** — each goal reads its status: **on track for Aug 2027** (green) or
  **behind — +£X/mo needed** (amber), with a progress bar and what it needs per
  month. Tap to set name, amount, date, and which savings line feeds it.
- **Income** — your take-home or salary. Tap to edit. Gross salaries are taxed
  automatically (2026/27 rUK); "Open full pay breakdown" shows tax/NI/pension and
  lets you add bonuses.
- **Outgoings** — grouped by category, collapsible, each group showing its monthly
  subtotal. Tap any line to edit it in place.
- **One-off costs** and **Variable spend** sit at the bottom (see below).

## Savings = an outgoing with a toggle

Add "Holiday savings" exactly like a bill, then flip **Savings**. It still reduces
your left-to-spend (the money's set aside), but it's reported as savings and
**accrues a balance** that grows at the global interest rate (set in Settings).
A **Target** is a separate, lightweight overlay (name, amount, date) that you can
link to a savings line — it tells you the required monthly amount and whether
you'll hit it in time.

## Entering everything (≈2 minutes)

1. Tap **Cash now**, set your balance.
2. **+ Income** → your salary (gross) or take-home (net).
3. **+ Bill** for each outgoing; set a category so they group.
4. **+ Savings** for money you set aside; give it a starting balance.
5. **+ Target** → "£4,000 by Aug 2027", link it to a savings line.
6. Watch the forecast and the target's hit-date. Use **Try a what-if** to play.

## Variable spend (optional)

Most bills are fixed, so there's no monthly logging chore. If you want to track a
couple of variable categories (groceries, fuel), open that bill and flip
**Variable — track spend**. Only those show up in the **Variable spend** panel for
the current month, with budget-vs-actual.

## Settings

Global savings interest rate, forecast horizon, currency, tax year, and the full
rUK tax table (so a new tax year is one edit). Opened from the ⚙️ icon.
